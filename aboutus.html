Tonnes of food were wasted daily in the messes of many academic institutes in India in their attempt to dine the residing students. As of July 2017, in IIT Roorkee itself, over 1000 kgs of food was wasted daily including fresh food as well. On the worst days, more than 200 kg of food is wasted in each mess after each meal. Compelled by this, we started a group called AAHAR : Action Against Hunger – A Revolution with the vision of eradicating hunger using the food which was nevertheless being thrown away. The idea was to collect the food left in different college messes and distribute it amongst the poor.

We started our initiative in August 2017, taking inspiration from the well known Robinhood Army - an organization famous for distributing food to the poor in the entire nation and beyond. We have been accompanied by around 100 volunteers to conduct more than 80 distribution drives in just over an year of operation - feeding of more than 6000 people. Even treacherous heats, torrential rains and freezing winters accompanying the extreme climate of Uttarakhand have been unable to halt us in our work.

We have also initiated the "Neki ki Peti" campaign wherein boxes are kept in students’ hostels to collect clothes to be distributed amongst these slums to endure harsh climatic conditions.
